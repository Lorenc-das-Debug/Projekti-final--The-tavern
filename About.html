Since 1942

A century at the same health

The Tavern opened its doors in the autumn of 1924, when Eamon Hartwood

converted the old grain mill on the river bend into a modest public house.

Travelers arrived on horseback. Locals gathered around the stone hearth that

still warms our dining room today.

Four generations later, the Hartwood family still tends the kitchen. Recipes

have been written, rewritten, and quietly perfected. The fire still burns. The

welcome is still warm.

We believe a good meal is more than what's on the plate. It's the room, the

company, the slow unwinding of an evening. We hope you'll join us soon —

and stay a while.
